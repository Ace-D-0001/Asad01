DESIGN OF A HIGHWAY SERVICE STATION

Year
2025
Category
Rest and Recharge Hub — Design of a Highway Service Station
Location
Panshi Restaurant Highway, Jogodishpur, Madhabpur, Sylhet
Academic Level
2nd Year, 2nd Semester

This project focused on redesigning the existing Panshi Highway Restaurant to create a more efficient and comfortable rest and recharge hub for travelers along the Sylhet highway. The design integrates essential highway facilities including a petrol pump for buses and private vehicles, 10 car parking spaces, and 4 bus parking bays, ensuring smooth circulation and accessibility. The restaurant was envisioned as a refreshing pause point, offering both functional and experiential value. A key design intervention was the introduction of an artificial waterbody beneath the restaurant structure, which helped in creating a pleasant microclimate through natural cooling and reflected light. The spatial layout was designed to promote visual openness, thermal comfort, and user relaxation, making it a welcoming stopover for highway users.

A Community Library

Year
2024
Category
Knowledge in Commons — A Community Library
Location
Korail Slum, Dhaka
Academic Level
2nd Year, 2nd Semester

This project focused on designing a community library for underprivileged children living in the Korail slum — a place where education often fades after the primary level. My aim was to create a space that would inspire children to dream, learn, and continue their education in an environment that feels joyful and welcoming. The concept of the design came from a toy that every child loves — the fidget spinner. Its circular, dynamic form symbolizes motion, play, and curiosity — qualities that I wanted to reflect in the library’s spaces. The design combines learning and play, offering areas where children can explore freely. Along with a reading zone, the project includes a multipurpose room for group activities, and a “wall of the future” that displays imaginative and futuristic visuals to encourage curiosity and hope. The goal was to create more than just a library — a safe and inspiring place where children can connect, imagine, and rediscover the joy of learning within their own community.

Cultural Expo

Year
2025
Category
Pavilion of Bengal Culture — Cultural Expo
Location
Beside Football Stadium, Funadhoo, Maldives
Academic Level
3rd Year, 1st Semester

This project was about designing a public exhibition pavilion that celebrates the vibrant spirit of Bengal’s traditional art forms — Alpona, Patachitra, and Rickshaw Art. The pavilion was envisioned as a space where visitors could experience the essence of Bengali culture through color, form, and storytelling. The concept of the design came from the idea of symmetry and circular harmony. I chose a circular massing to represent unity and continuity, where each circle served a distinct function. Three interconnected circular volumes were designed — each dedicated to a different art form — allowing every exhibition to have its own identity and display style while maintaining a cohesive overall composition. The project aimed to create a dynamic and interactive cultural space that not only exhibits art but also conveys the rhythm, emotion, and heritage of Bengal within a modern architectural language.
